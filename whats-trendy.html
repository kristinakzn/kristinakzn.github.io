What's Trendy

When preparing for a trip, be it for business or pleasure, decisions on what clothing, shoes and accessories will tag along with you can be tricky. No matter the climate or occasion, when the choices are made you’ll need a stylish suitcase to accompany your travels – and to house your wardrobe for the duration if there’s more than one destination on the list. The array of luxury luggage brands today all offer function as well as chic design, with collections ranging from elegant simplicity to vibrant, on-trend exteriors. Leading luggage lines span fashion heavyweights Louis Vuitton and Fendi to travel specialists such as Globe-Trotter. Designs also vary, with veteran luggage manufacturer Rimowa always at the forefront of trends by adopting the latest material technology from airplanes for its hard casing, where Gucci displays its signature motif with some added flair. Diverse shapes and sizes among the top luggage lines today offer something for every length of trip, including carry-on duffels, swiveling suitcases and all-encompassing trunks. Wherever you’re jetting off to, your suitcase should be as chic as it is secure. These are our picks of the best luxury luggage lines.

With roots in trunk making dating back as far as 1871, Louis Vuitton combines luxury with expert craftsmanship to provide the ultimate in sophisticated luggage. Whether you’re seeking the signature Monogram canvas, personalized details or colorful designs, globetrotters can’t go wrong with one of Louis Vuitton’s travel creations. The Horizon 55 line comes in a variety of exciting colored epi leather, like the Piment seen here, which can be customized with stripes and initials of choice. Owners of these pieces also have access to the LV Pass app, offering exclusive services.

Favored by jet setters and business travelers alike, Rimowa is known all over the world for its modern, ultra-lightweight luggage. In 1937, Rimowa became the first brand to manufacture aluminum trunks, drawing inspiration from the material and design of aircrafts. Since then, Rimowa has gone on to invent luggage made from polycarbonate – the same material aircraft windows are made of. The Salsa Deluxe collection (seen here) is stylish and secure, with the 3-Suiter featuring an add-a-bag holder, garment bag and hangers for added organization. The brand’s innovative electronic tag system eliminates the hassle of luggage check-in by allowing travelers to do it from home.
